Case assignment protocol — SCAI cases are forwarded to the Judicial Council by the Chief Justice or designee. Members are assigned based on availability and case type. Members must recuse themselves from any case where a conflict of interest exists per the Code of Conduct.

Council roster
Chief Justice — Presides over the full Council; delegates cases to ACJs or Associate Justices; acts as counsel to all committees (no vote unless breaking a tie); maintains training materials; notifies Council of all membership changes within 5 academic days.

External Assistant Chief Justice — Manages outreach and external communications; coordinates with the student body, SGLC/JLC, and UCF officials; assumes CJ responsibilities if Internal ACJ seat is vacant.

Internal Assistant Chief Justice — Oversees internal operations, committee coordination, and communication between the CJ and Associate Justices.

Associate Justices (Seats 1–14) — Voting members of the Judicial Council. Serve on appellate hearings, provide impartial advising, participate in judicial review, and serve on at least one standing committee.
Case topics
Impartial Advising
Justices serve as neutral guides for students navigating the SCAI conduct or grade appeal process. Advisory only — no hearing required.
Student Grade Appeal
Students disputing a final course grade. Justices may serve as advisors or panelists on the Grade Appeals Board.
Fiscal Administrative Appeal
RSOs or students appealing A&SF allocation decisions, budget freezes, or financial sanctions.
Election Violation Appeal
Challenges to SG candidate eligibility or alleged election code violations. Often expedited due to election timelines.
Student Conduct Appeal
Referrals from UCF's SCAI office for formal hearings involving Golden Rule or Student Code of Conduct violations.
Standing committees
Internal Rules Committee (IRC) — Reviews and proposes amendments to the Judicial Rules of Procedure.
Precedent Committee — Maintains and organizes the Council's case precedent library.
Judicial Experience Committee (JEC) — Coordinates professional development, study resources, and branch culture.
Judicial Outreach Committee (JOC) — Runs awareness programs educating the student body on their rights and SG judicial processes.
